# Workforce Skills and Talent Intelligence Methodology

## Boundary

The Python lab uses synthetic fictional workforce data. It is designed for research and portfolio demonstration, not real employment decisions.

## Core workflow

1. Generate employees, skills, projects, project skill demand, learning catalog, and contractor market.
2. Aggregate skill supply by capability and level.
3. Compare project demand to qualified internal people.
4. Score gap risk from shortage, project priority, deadline, and business value.
5. Estimate burnout risk from utilization, engagement, attrition signal, and tenure.
6. Recommend learning paths for employees close to critical gaps.
7. Compare contractor or hiring alternatives for urgent gaps.
8. Audit subgroup fairness signals.
9. Generate reports, figures, and a hash-chained audit log.

## Metrics

| Metric | Meaning | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Skill gap risk | Shortage-weighted project risk | Synthetic planning signal only |
| Burnout risk | Workload and engagement proxy | Not a medical or HR diagnosis |
| Learning recommendation | Course-path suggestion for skill gaps | Requires manager and employee review |
| Contractor alternative | Synthetic market option | Not procurement advice |
| Fairness gap | Group difference in utilization, burnout, and learning access | Audit signal requiring review |

## Human review

Real workforce recommendations must be reviewed by managers, HR, legal, and employees. The platform should not automate hiring, firing, promotion, compensation, performance, or health decisions.
